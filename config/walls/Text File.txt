Ah, I see the issue now! The logs show Waybar is initializing for both HDMI-A-1 and eDP-1.

On Wayland, you cannot have two instances of the StatusNotifierWatcher service running simultaneously. Since your config defines one bar that applies to "all" outputs, Waybar is trying to claim the tray path twice, which causes DBus to reject the second one and break the tray for both.

To fix this, you must explicitly tell Waybar to only render the tray on one primary monitor.

########

1. Update your config to separate outputs

Replace your current configuration with this. It splits the bars so the tray only initializes on your laptop screen (eDP-1), while the HDMI monitor gets a clean bar without a tray conflict.
